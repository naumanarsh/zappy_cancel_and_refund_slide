Cancelling a Plan & Giving Refunds

A simple explainer — two different things that are easy to mix up

Think of it like a Netflix subscription. Cancelling means “don’t charge me again next month” — but you still get to watch until the month you paid for runs out. A refund is a separate decision: actually giving money back. One does not automatically mean the other.
The key message: Cancelling is NOT the same as a refund. When a patient cancels, they keep their medication until the time they already paid for ends. Money only comes back if our team chooses to give a refund.

★ The five things to remember

  • 1Patients cancel by themselves; only staff give refunds. Everyone knows who’s responsible for what.
  • 2Cancelling means “stop renewing,” not “lose access.” Patients keep what they paid for until it runs out — fair, with no surprise loss of medication.
  • 3Refunds are real money back to the card, decided case by case, with a safety check so no one gets refunded twice.
  • 4Our records always match the payment system — they stay in sync automatically.
  • 5Everything is recorded, and the patient is always told — by email and text — whatever happens.
